peacock

The peacock—specifically the male Indian peafowl—is celebrated worldwide as the national bird of India, renowned for its hypnotic beauty and vibrant, iridescent plumage. Its spectacular courtship dance and deep cultural significance make it one of the most recognizable and majestic birds on the planet. Peafowl are native to the forests and open agricultural lands of South and Southeast Asia. They are largely ground-dwelling birds that forage for grains, seeds, insects, and small reptiles. Because they eat crop pests and venomous snakes, they are often considered highly beneficial to farmers. While they are capable of flight, they prefer to escape threats by running through the undergrowth, and they typically roost in tall trees at night to stay safe from predators.
